# Energy-System-for-an-Electrically-Independent-Greenhouse
The project develops a system based on the use of: Photovoltaic Panels and Micro Wind Turbines, sized and chosen according to the energy demand of a Heat Pump, which is responsible for the internal climate control of the greenhouse.

The system's operating logic follows a precise energy hierarchy: the energy converted by the photovoltaic and wind power systems is sent to power the heat pump when necessary.
When there is no electricity consumption by users, the electricity is directed to recharge the batteries, thus providing a reserve supply.
Once the maximum charge is reached, the residual energy is exported to the grid.
Considering the critical condition in which production and storage are insufficient to cover demand, a variable that counts the purchase of electricity from the grid is integrated into the simulation. Depending on the case, this will be used both to power the heat pump and to recharge the batteries.

It is clear to see how the power produced drastically exceeds the power required, indicating that the plant is significantly oversized in relation to actual requirements.
This oversizing can be advantageous from an economic point of view, as the greater the export quota, the greater the economic return. On the other hand, from a technical point of view, oversizing is a problem, as the idea of powering a greenhouse takes a back seat.
To explain: if the goal is to export as much electrical power as possible, then it makes little sense to consume usable space by placing a greenhouse instead of additional plants. In these terms, one could simply reduce the size of the plants or increase the size of the greenhouse. Or, one could change the location of the greenhouse, and therefore the plants, to climatic zones where greater energy demand is expected.
